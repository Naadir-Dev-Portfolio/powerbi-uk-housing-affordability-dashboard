# UK Housing And Affordability Dashboard Blueprint

This report is generated by `.build/build_report.py`. Do not manually rebuild the PBIR visual JSON files unless Power BI Desktop has changed the enhanced report format and the generator has been updated first.

## Page 1: Executive Summary

Purpose:
- Summarise the current UK house-price level, affordability pressure, Bank Rate context, and severe local authority pressure.
- Keep the first page numeric and data-driven, avoiding manually written narrative verdicts.

Main visuals:
- KPI cards: `UK Average House Price Latest`, `Purchase Ratio Latest`, `UK Bank Rate Latest At HPI Month`, `Severe Pressure Areas Count`.
- Trend line: annual purchase affordability for England, Wales, and England and Wales.
- UK country comparison: latest UK-method purchase affordability.
- Regional hotspot treemap and regional metrics matrix.

## Page 2: House Prices, Rates And Market Cycles

Purpose:
- Show house prices as a market series, not just affordability ratios.
- Connect monthly UK HPI, annual price change, Bank Rate, selected market stress periods, and a baseline forecast.

Main visuals:
- KPI cards: latest UK average house price, UK HPI index, annual price change, and Bank Rate at the latest HPI month.
- Line chart: UK average house price actuals plus trailing-24-month baseline forecast.
- Line chart: UK average house price path segmented by selected market stress period.
- Line chart: Bank Rate at monthly grain.
- Scatter chart: Bank Rate versus annual house-price growth by month.
- Diagnostics table: monthly price, HPI index, annual change, and Bank Rate.

## Page 3: Local Authority Pressure Explorer

Purpose:
- Let the viewer drill into local authorities and compare prices, incomes, affordability ratios, rent pressure, and monthly HPI context.

Main visuals:
- KPI cards: highest purchase pressure area, highest rental pressure area, severe pressure area count, and areas above 30% rent.
- Slicers: country, region, local authority.
- Scatter: latest local median earnings versus median house price, sized by purchase ratio.
- Table: local rank, latest house price, earnings, purchase ratio, and rental ratio.
- Monthly HPI basket trend and ranked local authority bar chart.

## Page 4: Income Distribution And Rental Strain

Purpose:
- Explain whether pressure is worse at the lower end of the distribution and whether rental pressure reinforces the same geography.

Main visuals:
- KPI cards: widest lower-quartile gap area, lower-quartile ratio, lower-quartile gap, rent above 30% share.
- Regional lower-quartile gap bar chart.
- Local median versus lower-quartile scatter, sized by rental pressure.
- Lower-quartile affordability trend by region.
- Local lower-quartile and rental detail table.

## Refresh Design

- Run `python "Source Data/scripts/download_uk_housing_affordability_data.py"` to fetch the latest public sources.
- Run `python "Source Data/scripts/prepare_housing_affordability_model_data.py"` to rebuild curated CSVs.
- Run `python .build/build_report.py` only after changing report layout logic.
- Run `python .build/validate_report.py` before opening the PBIP.

## Key Source-Grain Rules

- Annual affordability and earnings come from ONS and should stay annual.
- Monthly house-price movement comes from the UK HPI full monthly file.
- Bank Rate comes from the Bank of England IADB CSV endpoint for series `IUMABEDR`.
- Forecast values are a baseline projection from the trailing 24 months and should not be described as causal.
